## Core Brand Identity

### Mission
Democratizing AI by building decentralized training infrastructure and protocols that enable anyone to contribute to and benefit from frontier AI development.

### Vision
A world where AI is trained collectively by a global community, not controlled by a handful of corporations, ensuring AI benefits all of humanity equitably.

### Values
- **Decentralization**: Distributed consensus for collective intelligence
- **Democratization**: Open access to AI training and deployment
- **Community**: Global collaboration over corporate competition
- **Sustainability**: Efficient use of compute resources (DSO, PoAI)
- **Openness**: Open source code, open research, open governance

## Brand Positioning

**Target Audience**:
- AI researchers and enthusiasts
- Open source contributors
- DeFi and crypto communities
- Academic institutions
- Social impact organizations

**Key Differentiators**:
- 501(c)(3) non-profit foundation structure
- Decentralized Semantic Optimization (DSO) protocol
- Proof of AI (PoAI) consensus mechanism
- Co-development with Hanzo AI and Zen LM
- Focus on collective intelligence and experiential learning

## Visual Identity

### Logo Philosophy
The Zoo logo symbolizes diverse species (models/agents) thriving in an ecosystem through collaboration. The green palette represents growth, sustainability, and organic development.

### Color System
- **Emerald Green** (`#2ECC71`): Growth, nature, community
- **Forest Green** (`#27AE60`): Stability, trust, foundation
- **Amber Accent** (`#F39C12`): Energy, optimism, collaboration

### Typography
- **Inter**: Accessible, modern sans-serif for all communications
- **JetBrains Mono**: Technical documentation and code examples

## Usage Guidelines

### Do's ✅
- Use official logos from this repository
- Emphasize the 501(c)(3) non-profit nature
- Highlight community and decentralization themes
- Reference collaboration with Hanzo AI and Zen LM
- Link to zoo.ngo and zoo.ai as primary web presence

### Don'ts ❌
- Don't modify logo proportions or colors
- Don't imply commercial motives (we're a non-profit)
- Don't use logos without proper attribution
- Don't misrepresent the foundation's mission
- Don't use outdated branding materials
